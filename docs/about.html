Further

Why litigate against the rgortt?

Government will need to front the expenses for the cleanup and the overall restoration of the Lambeau coastline and Petit Trou lagoon - if other entities aren’t committed in a timely manner.

As articulated in the National Oil Spill Contingency Plan. (Section 6/Subsection 6.5. RECORD KEEPING AND PREPARATION OF CLAIMS (Page 46) Trinidad and Tobago have to make a claim to the International Oil Pollution Compensation Fund in accordance with the 1992 Protocol of the Convention on the Establishment of an International Fund for Compensation for Oil Pollution Damage.

This means there may be money coming in at some point and Lambeau needs to be positioned to collect some of it - And the only way to justify their cause, their right, is to make a credible case/ a scientific argument.

The litigation strategy is intended to keep the issue alive And to place the affected community at the top of the list for compensation. Obviously Government will extract its expenditure (fronted) towards the 2024 cleanup but the restoration should be managed by Tobago/ i.e. the Lambeau community.

What is unsaid is, Environment Tobago in empowering the cause of the Lambeau community is living up to its role as an advocate for environmental justice. Remember, Lambeau did not cause the February oil spill. They are simply victims of an injustice.

How does Environment Tobago benefit the organisation or it members in oil spill involvement, beyond ‘just’ simply cleaning coast and wiping oil of wildlife?

This NGO’s strategic plan provides guidance (or strongly suggests) that we need an item that can generate income for the operations of the group: Preferably in a niche that is not overly popular and therefore highly competitive.

The oil spill has occurred at time when Environment Tobago has to hand, capability to assess and guide the Lambeau community to a place less vulnerable to unplanned disaster. And to greater appreciation of the Risks they face from the rapid onset of changing climate, given their proximity to the open ocean.

In short, the opportunity provided by the oil spill is something ET is uniquely placed to capture. Which begins with implementing a Vulnerability Assessment.
