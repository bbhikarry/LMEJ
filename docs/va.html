Vulnerability Assessment

Vulnerability Analysis==VA

A tool for all seasons (climate humour) VA is a risk analysis process designed to engage with communities in contexts where disasters (and climate change) are significant drivers of poverty and suffering. VA’s are a tool for gauging resiliency of a community. VA’s use a plethora of devices to arrive at a result: Which we will discuss enroute to advocating for the community of Lambeau.

Some background. Environment Tobago had done a mini Vulnerability and Capacity Assessment (VCA) in the Lambeau/Petit Trou geography as part of the project titled “Action by Civil Society in Trinidad and Tobago to Build Resilience to Climate Change project (2015 - 2017), with support from Conservation International and BHP Billiton Trinidad and Tobago”.

More recently (Dec 2023) we participated in a 4 day workshop for 14 countries from the region to acquire skills as trainers of VCA trainers. Both sessions as well as others too numerous to mention were facilitated by the Caribbean Natural Resources Institute. From this writers perspective therefore, ET is capable of conducting the necessary works to gauge the impact of February’s disaster on the village of Lambeau.

After Feb 2024

Here is the overview of the LM4eJ programme. Please pardon the succinct nature of the following list but it is imperative the upcoming scenario is framed. Rest easy tho, details are provided within this site. We intend to

  1. collect data (quant/quant to inform our VA)
  2. generate information (again - for our VA)
  3. conducting analysis (to inform our VA and CCA and DDR policies)
  4. prioritise risk (in case there is a legal claim later )
  5. stimulate restorative action (ensuring community incl)
  6. move to ‘action’ (again with community in mind)

Generally the list above points to preparing a strategy for the village of Lambeau for untoward events that can hamper their ability to deal with/adapt to the onset of rapid climate change.

We shall defend our approach using the ‘natural forts’ theory/practise. Where each village or community should function as a resilient outpost by virtue of having strong ecosystems and functional natural services. In a word - Tobago’s resiliency to the effects of climate change is improved if each community is aware of and tending to its natural weaknesses and strengths.

Partipatory VA

VA toolkits are generally in agreement that the best way to elicit information and help from a community is via meaningful engagement. Enter the participatory VA.

However there are things the erstwhile VA ‘expert’ will want to touch on before putting boots ot the ground (as it were). Such as:

General Process and Scope:

  • How much time is available for the assessment?
  • On which areas do we want to focus the assessment?
  • Who will perform the assessment?
  • Do we really have the technical capacity to perform it in-house?
  • If outsourcing the assessment, what is the procedure that must be followed for hiring a contractor?
  • What questions do we want the assessment to answer?
  • Which specific decisions do you want the assessment to support?

Budget and Political Support:

  • What is our budget for the assessment?
  • Who will manage the assessment process and budget?
  • Will we have political support for the assessment?

Technical:

  • Will any model(s) be developed for the assessment?
  • Will the THA be able to operate the model for future scenario analysis?
  • What type of scenarios does the Lambeau community want to model?
    • (Best case?
    • Worst case?
    • Middle-of-the-road?

The above considerations are self explanatory (but not trivial) and require answering at Board level before deciding to saunter forth. In the meantime let’s get familiar with the elements of a participatory vulnerability assessment (PVA) with Lambeau in mind.
