Vulnerability Assessment

Vulnerability Analysis==VA

A tool for all seasons (climate humour) VA is a risk analysis process designed to engage with communities in contexts where disasters (and climate change) are significant drivers of poverty and suffering. VA’s are a tool for gauging resiliency of a community. VA’s use a plethora of devices to arrive at a result: Which we will discuss enroute to advocating for the community of Lambeau.

Some background. Environment Tobago had done a mini Vulnerability and Capacity Assessment (VCA) in the Lambeau/Petit Trou geography as part of the project titled “Action by Civil Society in Trinidad and Tobago to Build Resilience to Climate Change project (2015 - 2017), with support from Conservation International and BHP Billiton Trinidad and Tobago”.

More recently (Dec 2023) we participated in a 4 day workshop for 14 countries from the region to acquire skills as trainers of VCA trainers. Both sessions as well as others too numerous to mention were facilitated by the Caribbean Natural Resources Institute. From this writers perspective therefore, ET is capable of conducting the necessary works to gauge the impact of February’s disaster on the village of Lambeau.

After Feb 2024

Here is the overview of the LM4eJ programme. Please pardon the succinct nature of the following list but it is imperative the upcoming scenario is framed. Rest easy tho, details are provided within this site. We intend to

  1. collect quantitative and qualitative data to inform our VA
  2. generate information (again - for our VA)
  3. conduct analysis (to inform the our VA and THA’s CCA and DDR policies)
  4. prioritise risk (shape adaptive capacity thrust)
  5. stimulate restorative action (ensuring community inclusion)
  6. ‘action’ adaptive thrust (community focussed)

The list above points to preparing a strategy for the village of Lambeau to deal with for untoward events that can hamper its ability to adapt rapid climate change.

We want to build Tobago’s climate defense (adaptation strategy) via a ‘natural forts’ approach. Where each village or community should function as a resilient outpost; by virtue of having strong ecosystems and functional natural services.

In a word Tobago’s resiliency to the effects of climate change is improved if each community is aware of and tending to its natural weaknesses and strengths. In the jargon, Build Tobago’s capacity for Disaster and Risk Reduction (DDR) via sustainable Climate Change Adaptation (CCA) practice.
We’d need to identify a path to achieve those ‘forts’. Along the lines of; Identify problems >> Assess needs >> Secure environmental rights >> Train the community to >> Restore Reinforce and Rebuild natural and where possible >> Monetise.

Partipatory Vulnerability Assessments

VA toolkits are generally in agreement that the best way to elicit information and help from a community is via meaningful engagement. Enter the participatory vulnerability assessment or PVA.

There are things the VA team will want to touch on before putting boots to the ground. Such as determining:

General Process and Scope:

  • How much time is available for the assessment?
  • On which areas will ‘team’ want to focus the assessment?
  • Who will perform the assessment?
  • Does Environment Tobago have the technical capacity to perform it in-house?
  • If outsourcing the PVA what is the procedure that must be followed for hiring the contractor?
  • What questions do we want the assessment to answer?
  • Which specific decisions do you want the assessment to support?

Budget and Political Support:

The Board of Environment Tobago will need to determine:

  • What is the budget for the assessment?
  • Who will manage LM4eJ and oversee budget?
  • Will Environment Tobago have political support for project?

Technical == Scoping

  • Will a model be developed for the assessment?
  • Will the THA be able to operate the model for future scenario analysis?
  • What future scenario will the Lambeau community want to model?
    • Best case. A regional best example?
    • Worst case. Do nothing?
    • Middle of the road. Best Tobago example?

Some of the above considerations are self explanatory while some will require decisions at Board level. In the meantime let’s get familiar with the rudiments of a Participatory Vulnerability Assessment (PVA) keeping Lambeau in mind.
